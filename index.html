Our Mission

"We transform waste into wealth, and farms into clean energy hubs."

BioViL partners with rural communities to convert agricultural and household waste into biogas, reducing energy poverty, cutting toxic indoor pollution, and creating new income streams for farmers.

Our Vision

"Self-sustaining villages, resilient communities, and a planet that breathes."

We envision a world where every rural community has access to clean energy, not as charity, but as a right, driven by their own land, their own effort, and a shared stake in the outcome.

The Problem We Solve

India's Villages Deserve
Better Than Smoke.

Over 700 million people in rural India still cook with firewood or cow dung, breathing toxic smoke every day, while forests shrink and farmland sits under-utilised. BioViL was born from one insight: the waste already on your farm can fuel your future.

The Problem Today
  • Deforestation, forests shrink as firewood demand rises
  • Women's health crisis from toxic wood-smoke inhalation
  • Agricultural waste rotting unused on every farm
  • Expensive LPG pushing families deeper into poverty
  • Indoor air pollution linked to 1.6M deaths/year (WHO)
The BioViL Solution
  • Crop residue + animal dung = clean, safe biogas
  • Biogas feeds clean stoves, zero smoke, zero harm
  • Surplus energy sold, farms earn, not just spend
  • Digestate byproduct = rich organic fertilizer
  • Cooperative model, farmers become co-owners

Our Approach

A Three-Step Approach,
Built With Communities.

01

Educate

We run hands-on biogas workshops in villages, training farmers and households to understand the science, safety, and savings of anaerobic digestion. No jargon. Just results they can see and feel.

02

Engage

We don't just teach, we listen. Community surveys, youth programs, and feedback loops ensure our solutions fit real needs, not boardroom assumptions. BioViL is built with communities, not for them.

03

Implement

We build biogas systems using local materials and local knowledge, then train communities to run and maintain them independently. creating self-sustaining energy hubs that don't need us to survive.
